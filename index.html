Jimi Hendrix Tribute Page

Photo of The Essential Jimi Hendrix cassette tape.
November 27, 1942
James Marshal Hendrix, born Johnny Allen Hendrix in Seattle, WA.
1958
Jimi's father bought him his first acoustic guitar for $5 from one of his friends.
1959
Jimi's father bought him his first electric guitar (Supro Ozark 1560s).
1961
Hendrix enlisted in the Army. He was assigned to the 101st Airborn Division, stationed in Fort Campbell, Kentucky.
July 1962
Hendrix was honorably discharged after sustaining an ankle injury after his 26th Airborne jump.
1964-1965
Hendrix plays guitar in The Upsetters.(Little Rihard's back up band.)
September 1966
Hendrix meets Chas Chandler. (Manager and original bassist in The Animals)
Septemer 24, 1966
Hendrix moves to London.
Septermber 1966
The Jimi Hendrix Experience was formed in Westmister, London. Band members include Jimi Hendrix on guitar, Noel Redding bass, and Mitch Mitchell drums.
June 18, 1967
The Jimi Hendrix Experience plays at the Monterey Pop Festival in Monterey, California. Hendrix lights his guitar on fire and then smashes it. Rock and Roll!
July 16, 1967
The Jimi Hendrix Experience ended their tour opening for the Monkees after eight of their 29 scheduled tour dates. Fans of the Monkees pop sound did not understand or respect Jimi's new sound of rock. After being chastised by the audience, Hendrix flipped them off, threw down his guitar, and walked off stage.
August 23, 1967
Are You Experienced was released in America.
January 15, 1968
Axis: Bold as Love was released in America.
October 16, 1968
Electric Ladyland was released in America.
Jume 29, 1969
The Jimi Hendrix Experience disbanded. Noel Redding quit after a show in Denver.
August 18, 1969
Jimi Hendrix performs for nearly two hours at Woodstock.
March 25, 1970
Band of Gypsys was released in America.
September 18, 1970
At the age of 27, Jimi Hendrix aspirated his own vomit and died of asphyxiation while intoxicated with barbituates. There was no evidence of him taking any hard drugs at the time. The details of his final hours and death are unclear.
1992
The Jimi Hendrix Experience was inducted into The Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
